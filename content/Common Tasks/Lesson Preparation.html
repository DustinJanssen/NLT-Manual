Lesson Preparation

Preparing for your daily lessons primarily involves having the necessary tools and understanding of your class structure. Ensure to carry writing utensils, including at least a red and a blue marking pen. It's advisable to also bring a black pen, pencil, and eraser for versatility in marking and note-taking. Having a name list on hand can be beneficial, and is required on certain days like the class speech contest.

Split Classes

For split classes, your role will extend to designing lesson plans. The "We Can!" program you've used with other grade levels will be a valuable reference for this task. Despite Ms. Aoki creating two distinct levels for the grammar class, you are not obligated to do the same unless you're confident about it.

If you opt to use digital resources for teaching, bear in mind that OK House only has TVs for monitors. I personally leveraged my Mac and iPad to supplement the lack of a SMART board. Alternatively, you could make use of the English Department's tablet. While it does feature a touch screen, its performance can be sluggish, comparable to molasses on a cold winter morning. Therefore, patience might be required while operating it.
